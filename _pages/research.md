My interest in language contact was intially sparked by my fascination on how multiple languages coexist in time and space together. From there, work on pronoun borrowing (particularly [Abtahian, Cohn, Djenar, and Vogel (2021)](https://www.jbe-platform.com/content/journals/10.1075/aplv.20012.rav) and [Thomason and Everett (2001)](https://websites.umich.edu/~thomason/papers/pronborr.pdf)) grew my desire to learn more about how contact induces language change. I currently focus on how morphology (including clitics), pronouns, and other linguistic features can change when two or more languages interact. This has implications for bi- and multilingualism, creolistics, and language genesis and evolution. Ultimately, I think language contact can tell us so much about humankind's past, present, and future.
